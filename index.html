Bio

After completing a coding bootcamp, I have gained proficiency in three full stacks. I am now ready to bring my skills in C#/ASP.Net, MERN, and Python out into the real world.

Why Coding?
I got my first job as a dish washer in high school. 25 years later I have worked as a retail clerk, a security guard, a landscaper, a field technician, a teacher, a writer, and an data entry clerk meets account specialist. In every one of these jobs I found a common thread:they all had problems to solve.

In my adult life I've found joy in hobbies such as baking, gardening, knitting, and woodworking.

Coding allows me to bring the problem-solving skills I've honed in my professional life and marry them to the precision, patience, and attention to detail I've sharpened in my hobbies.

Accomplishments
Several years ago I had an opportunity to teach. Some of my students wanted books that would provide what the classes had offered. I had never written a book and I had certainly never published one, but at their request I set about the work. I searched out resources that would teach me how to format a book for printing, I shopped for a printer, and I distributed the books. The first was received so well that they asked me to do it twice more, so I complied. Each book was a little better, a little prettier, and a little easier to read than the previous one.I learned what I needed to do and kept trying to improve my processes.

As a data entry clerk I was handed a set of accounts that no one wanted to touch. They were too complicated, they had too many special rules, and the locations responsible for servicing the accounts had a reputation for being difficult to work with. Going into it, I had no idea of their reputation or that the accounts represented roughly 10% of the nationwide company's volume. I accepted those accounts, took about a week to learn the special rules that had been confounding my coworkers for months, and developed processes to handle their accounts with exceptional efficiency. (As a basis of comparison, the next largest set of accounts represented about 5% of the company's volume and it needed two people to barely stay on top of it.) Not only that, but I also built relationships with those "hard to work with" locations so that by the time I left they were not only among the most reliable locations in the company, but they were glad to hear from me when my office called.I saw the job that needed to be done and I made it work exceptionally well.

My Work Philosophy
I always try to work to the best of my ability. I always try to get better at what I do, even if what I am doing is just washing a dirty pan. If I don't know how to do something, I learn how to do it. If I can't find the answers on my own, I ask for help. If a co-worker or employer has feedback on the work I'm doing, I treat it as an opportunity to improve. I take my work far more seriously than I take myself.
